## 2. Zero-Tolerance Commitment

MatchXMatch has **zero tolerance for child sexual abuse and exploitation in any form**. Any user found to be engaging in, promoting, soliciting, distributing, or producing CSAE material — or attempting to do so — will have their account permanently terminated, their data preserved for law enforcement where applicable, and their conduct referred to competent authorities to the extent required by law.

We acknowledge that CSAE is a serious crime that causes profound harm to victims. Protecting minors from any sexualized contact, exposure, or solicitation on our platform is a non-negotiable part of how we operate.

## 3. Age Requirement — Strictly 18+

- MatchXMatch is **strictly for adults aged 18 and over**.
- Registration requires the user to provide a **date of birth**. Accounts where the stated date of birth indicates the user is under 18 are **not created**.
- If we become aware — through a report, a profile review, a chat review, or other means — that a registered user is under 18, the account is **immediately suspended and permanently deleted**, and any uploaded content is removed.
- We cooperate with parents, guardians, schools, and law enforcement seeking to confirm or remove an underage user's account.
- The App does not contain features designed to appeal to children. It is a dating product with adult-themed messaging and matching features.

If you believe a user on MatchXMatch is under 18, please report it immediately to **matchmatchx@gmail.com** with the username (if known) or use the in-app report mechanism.

## 4. Prohibited Content and Conduct

The following content and conduct are **strictly prohibited** on MatchXMatch. Violations result in immediate, permanent account termination and, where applicable, referral to law enforcement.

### 4.1 Content
- Any visual, textual, audio, animated, drawn, or computer-generated content that depicts, describes, or sexualizes a minor (a person under 18 years of age).
- Profile photos depicting minors in any context, including the user's own childhood photos.
- Links to, references to, or descriptions of CSAE material hosted elsewhere.
- Content that romanticizes, normalizes, or trivializes sexual contact with minors.

### 4.2 Conduct
- Soliciting, attempting to obtain, or offering CSAE material.
- Soliciting personal information, photos, or contact details from a person known or believed to be under 18 for any sexual or romantic purpose.
- Grooming behavior of any kind toward a person known or believed to be under 18.
- Misrepresenting one's own age to access adult content or interactions, or misrepresenting a minor as an adult.
- Attempting to use MatchXMatch to make contact with minors via shared social media handles, off-platform messaging, or any other channel.
- Threatening, extorting, or coercing any user with material that depicts a minor.

This prohibition applies to all parts of the platform: profiles, biographies, photos, chat messages, in-game text, social media links, usernames, feedback submissions, and any other user-generated content.

## 5. How Users Can Report CSAE

We provide multiple channels for reporting suspected child safety violations. All such reports are treated with priority.

### 5.1 In-App Reporting
- Every profile that becomes visible after a match exposes a **Report** action. Users can submit a report from the profile or chat screen.
- Reports are recorded with the reporter's identity (for follow-up), the reported user, and the match context.
- A user can also **block** any other user immediately, which cuts off all further contact regardless of report status.

### 5.2 Email Reporting
For reports that cannot be made in-app — for example, by a parent, a guardian, a member of the public, a child safety organization, or anyone who is not a MatchXMatch user — please email:

- **Address:** matchmatchx@gmail.com
- **Subject line:** `Child Safety Report — [brief description]`
- **Include if possible:** the username of the reported account, screenshots, the approximate date and time, and a description of what you observed.

Anonymous reports are accepted via email; please understand that we may not be able to give you a status update if you do not provide a reply address.

### 5.3 Reports From Law Enforcement and Child Safety Organizations
Law enforcement agencies, the U.S. National Center for Missing & Exploited Children (NCMEC), the UK Internet Watch Foundation (IWF), the Italian Postal Police's child protection unit (CNCPO), and equivalent agencies in other jurisdictions may contact us at the same email address. We will respond to lawful requests as quickly as practicable and prioritize requests involving imminent risk to a child.

## 6. How We Respond to CSAE Reports

When we receive a CSAE-related report — whether from a user, the public, or an authority — we follow this process:

1. **Triage.** Reports indicating CSAE or an underage user are prioritized over routine moderation queues.
2. **Account suspension.** The reported account is **suspended pending review** to prevent further contact while we investigate.
3. **Review.** A member of our team reviews the report, the account, and any associated content (profile, photos, messages where lawful to review). MatchXMatch operates with a small team and does not commit to a specific turnaround time, but child safety reports are handled as a top priority.
4. **Action.** If a violation is confirmed:
   - The account is **permanently banned** and all account-uploaded content is removed from public access.
   - The user's data and the offending content are **preserved** in accordance with applicable law to support any subsequent investigation.
   - Where the matter involves apparent child sexual abuse material, we will **report and/or hand over preserved material to competent authorities** as required by the law of the relevant jurisdiction (for example, mandatory reporting under U.S. 18 U.S.C. § 2258A where applicable, and equivalent obligations in the EU, the UK, Italy, and Turkey).
   - We cooperate with valid legal process — court orders, prosecutorial requests, and equivalent — and provide the data legally required to be produced.
5. **Closure.** We update the reporter where possible and reasonable. We do not disclose internal investigation details.

If a report does **not** substantiate a CSAE violation, we may still take other action (warning, restriction, termination) under our general Community Standards, or close the report with no action.

## 7. Preventive Measures Currently in Place

We are transparent about what we do and what we do not do. Misrepresenting our safeguards would itself be a breach of trust with our users and regulators.

**Currently in place:**
- **Age gate at registration.** Date of birth is required, and accounts indicating under-18 are not created.
- **Anonymous chat by default.** Profile photos and identifying information remain hidden until both parties have exchanged 20 messages each, reducing the surface for unsolicited photo exchange with strangers.
- **Per-user report mechanism.** Every account is reportable; a user is limited to one active report against a given other user (to deter abuse of the report system), and may always report serious safety issues by email.
- **Block mechanism.** Any user may unilaterally block any other user.
- **Karma / reputation tracking.** An internal trust score reflects user reports and behavior over time; degraded scores increase the priority of account review.
- **Human review of escalations.** Reports are reviewed by a person, not by an automated decision system, before any permanent action is taken.
- **Account deletion.** Users may delete their account and all associated data from in-app Settings or by email at any time.
- **Server-side legal hold.** Where law requires it, we can preserve account data and content for an investigation rather than honor a deletion request.

**Not currently in place (and not claimed):**
- We do **not** currently operate automated CSAM image-hash matching (e.g., PhotoDNA) on uploaded photos. Reports are reviewed manually. We are evaluating provider integrations and will update this document when our capability changes.
- We do **not** currently use automated scanning of chat messages for CSAE indicators.
- We do **not** currently require government-ID or selfie-based age verification beyond the self-declared date of birth at signup.

These honest disclosures will be updated as our capabilities evolve.

## 9. Compliance With Applicable Law

MatchXMatch complies with applicable child protection laws in the jurisdictions where the App operates, including without limitation:

- **United States:** 18 U.S.C. § 2258A (reporting of CSAM to NCMEC by electronic communication service providers, as applicable).
- **European Union:** the Digital Services Act (Regulation (EU) 2022/2065); Regulation (EU) 2024/1307 (extending the temporary derogation for the voluntary detection of CSAM on number-independent interpersonal communications, as applicable); and EU member-state criminal law on CSAE.
- **Italy:** Law 269/1998 and Law 38/2006 criminalizing the production, distribution, possession, and procurement of child pornographic material; and the supervisory framework of the Italian Postal Police (Polizia Postale e delle Comunicazioni — CNCPO).
- **United Kingdom:** Protection of Children Act 1978; Criminal Justice Act 1988, s. 160; Sexual Offences Act 2003; Online Safety Act 2023.
- **Turkey:** Law No. 5237 (Turkish Penal Code) Article 103 and related provisions; Law No. 5651 on regulation of internet publications.
- **Other jurisdictions:** local equivalent legislation in any country where the App is available.

Where the law of a user's jurisdiction imposes additional or stricter obligations than those stated here, that law governs.
